Register as a building inspector

Use this service to apply to become a registered building inspector with the Building Safety Regulator (BSR). You must register with us to carry out BSR-regulated activities (for example, assessing plans and inspecting). This includes for all buildings from domestic premises to complex, high-risk, multi-use structures. We will publish a register of building inspectors. The register will include your name, your employer, and details of BSR-regulated activities you are registered to do. We will tell you in the application which details will be published. Your registration will be valid for 4 years, unless varied or cancelled by us. If you register before 1 April 2024, the 4 years begins from 1 April 2024.

What you will need to register

To register, we will ask you to provide information about:
  • yourself, such as name, home address, email, and phone number
  • the class of building inspector you are applying to register as
  • details of your BSR-approved competency assessment
  • membership of relevant professional bodies
  • your employment status
We will ask you to confirm that you will abide by our code of conduct for registered building inspectors. We charge £336 for each registration, plus £216 annual maintenance payable from the first anniversary of your registration. Registration is for a maximum of 4 years.

Before you start

Read the guidance about classes of registered building inspector to decide which class you will register for. Use a BSR-authorised scheme to certify your competency, unless you are registering as a class 1 building inspector. Read the guidance about:
  • Registering as a building inspector
  • the role of building inspectors under the Building Safety Act 2022
  • Our code of conduct for registered building inspectors.
